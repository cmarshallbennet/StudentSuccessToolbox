Use

In producing assignments you will draw on a range of sources – course units, textbooks, the Internet, journal articles, newspapers, databases – to develop and inform your ideas. These ideas belong to those that wrote them (or spoke them, recorded them etc.). Therefore, when you draw on those ideas in your work you have to clearly show where they came from. You do this by including what are called references in your work. Properly referencing, showing where you have used the work of others, lies at the heart of all high quality academic work.

Eamon (Tutor)

What is Referencing

Indicates where you found the information you use in your work

Acknowledges other people’s work

Why do we Reference?

Shows where you sourced information

Displays the extent of your research

Helps back up your arguments

Avoids charges of Plagiarism
